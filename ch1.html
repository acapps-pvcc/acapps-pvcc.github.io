Three misconceptions about Computer Science:
  1. Computer Science is the study of computers.

  2. Computer Science is the study of how to write computer programs.

  3. Computer Science is the study of the uses and applications of computers and software.
Briefly define:
  1. Computer Science: The study of algorithms, their formal and mathematical properties, hardware realizations, linguistic realizations, and their applications.

  2. algorithm: A procedure for solving a mathematical problem in a finite number of steps that frequently involves repetition of an opera tion; broadly: a step-by-step method for accomplishing some task.

  3. sequential operation: A sequential instruction carries out a single well defined task. When that task is finished, the algorithm moves on to the next operation. Sequential operations are usually expressed as simple declarative sentences.

  4. conditional operation: Are the “question-asking” instructions of an algorithm. They ask a question, and the next operation is then selected on the basis of the answer to that question

  5. iterative operation: Are the "looping" instructions of an algorithm. insted of go to the next instruction it go back repeat the execution of a previous block of instructions.

  6. stored program computer: It is one of the early computers built by the RAND Corp.

  7. programmer: One who solve a problem on a computer and write commands in a high-level language.

  8. integrated circuit: With transistors, resistors, and capacitors were photographically etched onto a piece of silicon, which further reduced the size and cost of computers

  1. First generation computer: It is called vacuum tubes comuters, which is built for sale, and the IBM 701. This type of computer were bulky, expen sive, slow, unreliable, and extremely difficult to maintain.

  2. Second generation computer: The single transistor is a replacement for vacuum tube, and it is dramatically reduced the size of computers but also increased their reliability and reduced costs.

  3. Third generation computer: It is the era of the intgrated circuit, which further reduced the size and cost of computers.

  4. Fourth generation computer: This generation introduced the first microcomputer using very-large-scale integrated circuits that have advanced to a single circuit board that could be advanced hand. It also included further reduction in size and cost, increased reliability, and included new graphical user interfaces with pull-down menus, icons.

FORTRAN:
  1. What does the acronym FORTRAN stand for? It stands for "Formula Translation".

  2. When was FORTRAN developed? FORTRAN is developed in 1957.

  3. What was it used for? FORTRAN is used for numeric and scientific computing.

  4. Is FORTRAN still being used today? If so, what for? Yes, FORTRAN is still used in today. It is used for High-Performance Computing (HPC), scientific computing and engineering computations.
COBOL:
  1. What does the acronym COBOL stand for? COBOL stands for "Common Business Oriented Language"

  2. When was COBOL developed? COBOL is developed in the year of 1959.

  3. What was it used for? COBOL is used in business, finance, and administrative systems for companies and governments.

  4. Is COBOL still being used today? If so, what for? Yes, COBOl is still used in today. It is used for powering critical systems in industries such as finance, healthcare, and government.
